The High Cost of Hindsight

Traditional slope stability monitoring is a constant battle against overwhelming data, environmental challenges, and the unpredictable nature of geological failures. Current methods are fundamentally reactive, catching problems only as they escalate and providing analysis after a failure, not the critical warning needed to save lives.

Traditional methods are post-event, analyzing failures only after they happen.

Monitoring systems often fail due to human error, not technology.

Data Overload

Millions of data points create noise, making it nearly impossible for humans to spot subtle, critical precursor trends.

Sensor Degradation

Harsh environments (dust, rain) degrade sensor accuracy, leading to missed data or false positives.

The "Human Factor"

Failures are often due to interpretation errors or delayed responses. Automation is key.

Reactive Alarms

Threshold-based alarms trigger too late to safely evacuate personnel and equipment.

Case Study: The North Wall Incident

Averted Disaster at Mine Site Alpha

At a major copper mine, traditional monitoring showed minor displacement on the North Wall. However, Sentinel AI, running in a pilot program, flagged a 78% probability of a significant wedge failure within 48 hours. The model had detected a subtle correlation between rising pore water pressure and micro-accelerations invisible in the raw radar data.

The mine enacted its TARP based on our prediction. 36 hours later, a 20,000 cubic meter rockfall occurred. All personnel and equipment had been cleared, preventing fatalities and saving an estimated $12M.
